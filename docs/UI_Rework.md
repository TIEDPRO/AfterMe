# UI Rework

Product and UI direction: prioritise **time-to-value** and **truth in the UI**, then align monetisation so it feels intentional rather than accidental. The items below build on a review of friction in onboarding, the safety-net step, premium gates, and vault progress messaging.

---

## 1. Get people to a useful vault faster

Collapse or reorder onboarding so a new planner hits **one short “why you’re here” moment**, then **legal + biometrics** (unavoidable), then **the main app**. Move long education (values, contacts story, category philosophy) into **contextual prompts inside the vault** or a skippable “guided setup” path—not a mandatory tunnel before they’ve stored a single document.

---

## 2. Fix the safety-net moment so actions match words

Do not let **“Create a Family Kit”** complete onboarding with only a deferred flag. Either:

- **Open the kit flow immediately** after that choice (with a clear “you can finish after adding documents” inside the wizard), or  
- Reframe the card as **“I’ll create a kit next”** and make the next screen explicitly **“Add your first document”**, then kit.

Apply the same discipline to the **vault safety-net banner**: if copy mentions **backup or kit**, the tap should **offer both** (e.g. a sheet with two actions) or the copy should **only** promise what the tap does.

---

## 3. One rule for premium and Family Kit

Pick a single rule—for example **wizard always allowed, export/share paywalled**, or **wizard behind paywall everywhere**—and apply it consistently from **Vault**, **Family Kit tab**, and **Settings**. Mixed gates read as a bug and erode trust.

---

## 4. Align progress with the free tier

Avoid pushing **“24 key documents”** as the primary story for users who can only keep **five** without paying. Show **honest progress** (e.g. “3 of 5 on your current plan” or “Core set complete — unlock the rest”) so ambition and limits do not fight each other.

---

## 5. Soften defer without hiding risk

Replace playful shame (**“Living dangerously”**) with **neutral copy**, a **single clear risk line**, and **“Remind me later.”** A **confirmation on defer** can remain; tone does not need to pile on.

---

## 6. Clarify the first screen

Visually or verbally separate **“New vault”** from **“I already have a kit / backup”** so the default path is obvious and the other paths feel **advanced**, not three equal-weight buttons.

---

## 7. Only promise trials you implement

Today the app enforces a **document-count free tier**, not a **time-based trial** in code. Either **implement** a real trial with dates and rules, or **avoid implying** a time trial in copy and lean on **“free to start, upgrade when it matters.”**

---

## Suggested sequencing

**Near term (high leverage, relatively contained):**

- Banner / tap alignment (copy matches actions).  
- Honesty for kit vs onboarding completion.  
- Single premium rule for Family Kit across entry points.  
- Progress messaging vs free-tier limits.

**Next:**

- Shorten or modularise onboarding.  
- Contextual education and an optional **full guided path** for users who want more hand-holding.

---

## Rationale (short)

**Trust and clarity** are cheap to improve and make it easier to design a simpler end-to-end flow—including any future yes/no setup you choose—without fighting existing mismatches between what the UI promises and what the app does.
